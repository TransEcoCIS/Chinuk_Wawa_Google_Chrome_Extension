# Chinuk_Wawa_Google_Chrome_Extension
This extension translates all website text into Chinuk Wawa (Chinuk Jargon). Current capabilities are severely limited.

- About the Project
1. About me:
I was born and raised in the Pacific Northwest. I love everything about it. So much in fact that I am studying for my Environmental Science degree to help preserve it eventually. I'm also deeply political, and it seems that our government is adamant about erasing history, especially with Native American culture. Renaming the Denali Mountain, clearing native land for impromptu detention facilities, federal document censorship of the word "tribal", rewriting or banning Smithsonian exhibits that talk about Native American suffrage, and selling public land and waters, just to name a few, all in the name of alleviating a supposed "sense of national shame".
2. How it Came to be:
So, to preserve the past and to respect those who lived here before me, I wanted to learn the language spoken here. That'd be the Kalapuyan language, which has already effectively been mostly erased from history. I believe there are some translation dictionaries out there, but they are not available to the public.
That's when I learned that the Kalapuyan people likely spoke, or at least understood, another language used for trading up and down the Pacific Northwest. Chinuk Wawa is more documented and available to the public, but it still seems to be a rare and dying language. 
With that in mind, I set to work on trying to teach myself by dictionary alone. Obviously, that's all well and fine, but it's not grammatically accurate. I could translate "the man speaks" as "man wáwa," but without grammatical accuracy, it'd translate to man's language instead of what I meant to write (man yáka wáwa, the man talks). I needed someone, or something, to teach me. Normally, this would be Duolingo or a similar software, but I couldn't easily find one of these. Next best thing, use Google Translate. Sure, it might be iffy here and there, but at least it'd be something. Except, it's not on Google Translate either. It's nowhere.
If you want a job done right, you have to do it yourself. I will learn through digital immersion, but how?
3. Note:
I hope this extension doesn't get taken as culturally insensitive. I have no plan to profit from this project at all. I just want to have my name attached to it so I'll be credited as the creator. I want it to be free and available for everyone to use. We live in America, but only very few of us know how to speak the languages of the land. Imagine being born in France, living in it for 26 years, and then saying, "Yeah, I don't actually know what people are saying. I think it's Italian. They should really learn to speak Japanese here." IDK, seems really dumb, but I'm a short-minded person at times.
If you like what I am doing here, then I beg you to please share it with others. I've had it with the erasing of all minorities.

- Installation Instructions
1. Download contents.js and manifest.json.
2. Extract the Files -
        a. Make a dedicated file in your Documents (or wherever), name it "Chinuk Wawa Google Chrome Extension"
        b. Put the downloaded js and json in it
        c. Make sure the files names aren't changed. They should be: contents.js & manifest.json.
3. Import the Files -
        a. Open Chrome, click on the 3 dots (vertically inline) in the top right, click on "Extensions", and then on "Manage Extensions"
        b. In the top right corner, enable developer mode by clicking on the slider.
        c. Click "Load Unpacked" in the top left corner
        d. Select the file Chinuk Wawa Google Chrome Extension file and click "Select Folder"
